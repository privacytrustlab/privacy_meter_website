A privacy auditing library for all!

It's never been easier to audit the privacy of an algorithm, by using built-in features or by plugging-in your own custom metric.

Get it Contribute Documentation

Privacy Meter is an open-source library to audit data privacy in statistical and machine learning algorithms. The tool can help in data protection impact assessment process by providing a quantitative analysis of fundamental privacy risks of a (machine learning) model. It uses state-of-the-art inference techniques to audit a wide range of machine learning algorithms for classification, regression, computer vision, and natural language processing. Privacy Meter generates extensive reports about the aggregate and individual privacy risks for data records in the training set, at multiple levels of access to the model.

Compatible with major ML frameworks

Features

Check out our list of awesome features below.

Various inference games

Privacy Meter supports auditing of the average privacy loss of a training algorithm, and of the privacy loss of a model. Privacy loss of a data record and worst-case privacy loss of a training algorithm are coming soon.

Easy integration

Privacy Meter supports many common libraries and workflows, so that you can use it out of the box. Your favorite library is not supported? Our wrapper classes will make it work within minutes.

Reports generation

Easily create complete PDF reports to share with your team, or beautiful plots for your next paper.

Built-in metrics

Out of the box metrics include the population metric, reference metric, and shadow metric. White-box metrics are coming soon.

Customizable algorithms

The library is made of independent and interoperable classes, meaning you can easily overwrite one of them to change a specific aspect of the audit.

Many more

The Privacy Meter library is still a work in progress, so you can expect additional features soon!

Which libraries are compatible?

Out of the box, Privacy Meter is compatible with Tensorflow, Pytorch, HuggingFace, and OpenVino. Many other libraries can be used using a wrapper class.
